Citrus Pectin

**Citrus Pectin**: Citrus Pectin is a soluble fiber found in the peels of citrus fruits. It plays a pivotal role in promoting satiety, helping individuals feel full for longer periods. Fucoxanthin

**Fucoxanthin**: Extracted from brown seaweed, Fucoxanthin is a carotenoid that has been linked to fat burning properties. 4

Vitamin E

**Vitamin E**: An essential fat-soluble vitamin, Vitamin E is known for its antioxidant properties. It helps protect the body's cells from damage caused by free radicals, supports immune function, and promotes skin health. Additionally, Vitamin E plays a role in reducing inflammation in the body.
5

Vitamin B Complex

**Vitamin B Complex**: The Vitamin B Complex comprises multiple B vitamins, each contributing to various aspects of health. Collectively, they support energy production, red blood cell formation, and proper nerve function. B vitamins also play a role in converting food into energy, aiding metabolism, and promoting healthy skin and hair.
6

Resveratrol

**Resveratrol**: Found in the skin of red grapes and in red wine, Resveratrol is an antioxidant compound associated with anti-aging benefits. It has been studied for its potential in supporting heart health, reducing inflammation, and aiding in fat loss. Resveratrol may also play a role in activating certain genes that prevent cellular aging.
7

Black Pepper

**Black Pepper**: More than just a common kitchen spice, Black Pepper contains a compound called piperine, which can enhance the absorption of certain nutrients in the body. It also has potential metabolism-boosting properties and can aid in the digestion of food.
8

Dandelion

**Dandelion**: Dandelion is a versatile herb known for its diuretic properties, helping to reduce water retention in the body. It also supports liver health, aids digestion, and provides a rich source of certain vitamins and minerals, including potassium.
9

Beetroot

**Beetroot**: Beetroot is a nutrient-dense vegetable known for its potential in improving blood flow and reducing blood pressure. It contains nitrates, which the body converts into nitric oxide, a molecule that dilates blood vessels and improves circulation.
10

Citrus Bioflavonoids

**Citrus Bioflavonoids**: Derived from citrus fruits, Citrus Bioflavonoids are compounds that exhibit antioxidant properties. They work in tandem with Vitamin C to support immune function, promote healthy skin, and protect cells from oxidative stress.
