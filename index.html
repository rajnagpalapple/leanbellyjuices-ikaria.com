Citrus pectin

Citrus pectin is a natural fiber derived from the peels of citrus fruits. It is known for its ability to bind to cholesterol in the gut, potentially reducing cholesterol absorption and promoting heart health. Additionally, citrus pectin may support digestive health by acting as a prebiotic, feeding beneficial gut bacteria.

African mango extract

African mango extract, sourced from the seeds of the African mango tree, has gained popularity as a weight loss supplement. Studies suggest that it can help reduce body fat, improve cholesterol levels, and stabilize blood sugar levels. Its potential appetite-suppressing properties make it a sought-after ingredient in weight loss formulations.
3

Milk thistle

Milk thistle is a herb renowned for its liver-protective properties. The active compound in milk thistle, silymarin, has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. It aids in detoxifying the liver, supports liver function, and can help protect the liver from damage caused by toxins or diseases.
4

Resveratrol

Resveratrol is a natural compound found in grapes, red wine, and certain berries. It is known for its antioxidant properties, which can help combat oxidative stress in the body. Research has shown that resveratrol can support heart health, improve insulin sensitivity, and even extend lifespan in certain organisms.
5

Bioperine

Bioperine is an extract derived from black pepper. It is primarily used to enhance the bioavailability of other nutrients and compounds, meaning it helps the body absorb and utilize them more effectively. By increasing the absorption rate of other ingredients, Bioperine ensures that supplements are more potent and effective in delivering their promised benefits.
